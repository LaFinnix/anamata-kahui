# Prime Minister's Scholarship for Asia — research summary

**Source:** Research subagent report, 2026-07-22.
**Sources consulted:** Education New Zealand's official PMS site (`scholarships.enz.govt.nz`), archived individual + group terms, and the 2022 individual assessment criteria PDF.

---

## TL;DR

**The programme is discontinued.** Education New Zealand Manapou ki te Ao states:

> "The programme was discontinued, and its funding reprioritised in the latest Government Budget 2025."

The change took effect **1 July 2025**. ENZ continued supporting existing recipients only until **30 June 2026**.

**There is no 2026 application round or deadline.** Do not list PMSA as an active funding opportunity in any tracker or application.

---

## Official programme structure

Two streams only:

1. **Prime Minister's Scholarship for Asia (PMSA)** — Japan included
2. **Prime Minister's Scholarship for Latin America (PMSLA)**

**No ENZ Prime Minister's Scholarship for Europe exists.** Europe references conflate it with other schemes.

Asia destinations (official list): Greater China (incl. Taiwan and Hong Kong), Singapore, India, **Japan**, Republic of Korea, Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia, The Philippines, Thailand.

---

## Historical funding amounts (NZD)

### Individual scholarship components
- Tuition fees for course in Asia / Latin America
- Approved internship-provider brokerage fees: up to **$530 per programme week**
- Return economy flight contribution:
  - NZ → Asia: **$3,500**
  - NZ → Latin America: **$4,500**
- Living + accommodation contribution:
  - Asia: **$470 per active programme week**
  - Latin America: **$380 per active programme week**
- Travel + medical insurance
- Visa contribution: up to **$200**

### Asia living-cost totals
| Programme | Weeks | Asia living/accom contribution |
|---|---:|---:|
| One-semester exchange/study abroad | 18 | **$8,460** |
| Two-semester exchange | 36 | **$16,920** |
| Full-year academic | 36 | **$16,920** |
| Two-year academic | 72 | **$33,840** |

### Group programme
- Additional funding of up to **$20,000** at panel discretion
- Minimum **3 participants**
- Scholarships awarded to **individual participants**, not the organisation

---

## Programme lengths

- Minimum: **6 weeks** (Asia) / 4 weeks (Latin America)
- Internship: up to 6 months
- Exchange / non-exchange study: 1 or 2 semesters
- Whole postgraduate programme: up to 2 years
- Maximum funded study time: **72 weeks over 24 months**
- Programme had to begin within 20 months of the award

---

## Historical eligibility for Anamata-style work

A "research" category was official. The eligibility test:

> "Undertake short term research in one of our eligible destination countries as part of a postgraduate New Zealand qualification."

A NZ-based researcher going to Japan for fieldwork **was potentially eligible** if:
- Japanese work was short-term research
- Formed part of a **postgraduate NZ qualification**
- Japan was the approved destination
- Programme lasted ≥ 6 weeks
- Host institutional support secured
- Met programme's international-connection / community / national-benefit goals

### Discipline fit (no closed list — open to any qualifying subject)
| Proposed area | Fit |
|---|---|
| Cross-border/cross-cultural research | Strong |
| Indigenous comparative studies | **Strong and evidenced** — Māori–Ainu/Japanese exchange was funded |
| Indigenous music | Potentially eligible (framed as postgraduate research) |
| Music technology | Potentially eligible (no disciplinary exclusion) |
| Cultural preservation | Potentially strong |
| Language revitalisation | Potentially eligible |
| Cultural archive / digital preservation | Potentially eligible |
| Commercial touring / recording only | **Poor fit** — programme funded education, not commercial release activity |

---

## Eligibility (citizenship)

- NZ citizen **OR** NZ permanent residence
- Ordinarily resident in NZ for at least **12 of the preceding 24 months**
- No general maximum age
- Programme could not already have started when award made
- Virtual programmes not eligible

## Selection criteria — 50 points total

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Recipient + programme goals | **20** |
| Community | **15** |
| Institutional / workplace | **5** |
| National | **5** |
| Diversity | **5** |

### Māori / Pasifika / Tiriti alignment
- No numerical quota
- Māori + Pasifika explicitly in "under-represented demographic" — worth **3 of 50 points**
- Te Tiriti o Waitangi commitments built into assessment criteria
- **No explicit "Vision Mātauranga" language** in official criteria — don't claim formal alignment

---

## Required outputs

- Evidence of programme completion
- Reports / surveys on request
- Possible promotional + alumni participation

**Not prescribed:** paper, conference, publication. Outputs arose from the underlying qualification or host programme, not the scholarship itself.

## Implications for Anamata Kāhui

### Live status
- **NOT eligible to apply** — programme closed
- **Historical eligibility:** subject matter plausibly qualified (postgrad NZ research OR 3-person group exchange)

## What a future "Research papers" section should surface (per archived PMSA criteria)

To make platform research legible to similar funders, record for every paper:

1. Title + abstract
2. Research question + discipline
3. Researcher names, iwi/hapū, institutions
4. NZ qualification + supervisor
5. Host country, host institution, local/Indigenous partners
6. Programme type: fieldwork / exchange / postgrad / internship / short course
7. Start/end dates + weeks overseas
8. Ethics approval + community permissions
9. Mātauranga Māori / Indigenous knowledge governance
10. IP / copyright / consent / withdrawal / data sovereignty conditions
11. Languages used + revitalisation outcomes
12. Outputs: thesis, paper, dataset, recordings, archive, exhibition, conference, workshop
13. Community dissemination + knowledge-return plan
14. Institutional + workplace benefits
15. National + international benefits
16. Reciprocal network + partnership outcomes
17. Funding source + award identifier
18. DOI, ORCID, repository links, citation metadata
19. Public-access level + cultural-access restrictions
20. Outcome metrics + post-programme report

This mirrors PMSA's strongest scoring areas: programme fit, community contribution, mutual cultural understanding, knowledge sharing, institutional benefit, national benefit.
