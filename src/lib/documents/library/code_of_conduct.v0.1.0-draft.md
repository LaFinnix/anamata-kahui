# Code of Conduct

This Code of Conduct applies to every artist on the Anamata Kāhui
roster, regardless of branch, role, or contract type.

> **Note:** This is a draft document. The text below is placeholder
> legal copy that establishes the structure; it has not been reviewed
> by counsel and is not legally binding. Real legal text will replace
> this draft before the first artist is asked to sign.

## 1. Whakapapa — Origin and belonging

We acknowledge that every artist on the roster carries knowledge,
story, and lineage that did not begin with us. Your whakapapa precedes
your contract and continues after it.

## 2. Cultural authority

You are responsible for the cultural accuracy of what you create.
Where your work draws on the knowledge, pūrākau, or tikanga of an iwi
or hapū other than your own, you commit to seeking that iwi's input
before recording or releasing.

## 3. Kaitiakitanga — Stewardship

You commit to treating the platform, your collaborators, and the
communities your work serves with care. The platform is a kaitiaki
relationship, not a market.

## 4. Right of withdrawal

You may at any time request the withdrawal of any of your work from
the platform. We commit to processing such requests within thirty (30)
days, in line with the platform's CARE-aligned data governance
principles.

## 5. Disclosure of conflicts

If a real or perceived conflict arises between your obligations to
this roster and your obligations elsewhere — to an iwi, to another
label, to a community — you commit to disclosing that conflict in
writing before continuing affected work.

## 6. No retaliation

You will not be penalised for raising concerns about the platform,
about another roster member, or about Anamata Kāhui's partners. Good
faith concerns are protected.

## 7. Breach and remedy

A breach of this Code may result in a pause of roster membership,
pending an independent review. The review will be conducted by a
kaitiaki rōpū that does not include the party who raised the concern.
